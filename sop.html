Trooper Standard Operating Procedures

Trooper SOP – Chain of Command

  • Always follow the chain of command.
  • Obey lawful orders from higher-ranking officers.
  • Disrespect or disobedience will result in discipline.

Trooper SOP – General Duties

  • Enforce traffic laws and maintain highway safety.
  • Respond to accidents, crimes, and emergency calls.
  • Assist local law enforcement when needed.
  • Always file reports after incidents.

Trooper SOP – Conduct

  • Stay professional and respectful at all times.
  • No FailRP, RDM, VDM, or abuse of powers.
  • Always roleplay actions properly (e.g., -cuffs- before cuffing).
  • Bodycam/Dashcam must be on if server requires it.

Trooper SOP – Uniform & Gear

  • Full Trooper uniform must be worn on duty.
  • Standard gear: radio, firearm, taser, handcuffs, flashlight, vest, issued vehicle.
  • Make sure all equipment is working before patrol.

Trooper SOP – Vehicle Rules

  • Drive responsibly unless in pursuit/emergency.
  • Lights/sirens only when needed.
  • End pursuits if ordered by higher-up or if unsafe.

Trooper SOP – Arrest & Detain

  • Must have proper reason before arresting.
  • Always state charges to the suspect.
  • Cuff, search, and transport safely.
  • Read rights if required by server rules.
  • File an incident report.

Trooper SOP – Use of Force

  • Start with verbal commands.
  • Use taser or non-lethal first if possible.
  • Firearm/lethal only if life is in immediate danger.
  • Report all uses of force.

Trooper SOP – Radio & Comms

  • Keep radio clear and professional.
  • Use unit call signs (e.g., 2-45 on scene).
  • Respond to dispatch quickly.

Trooper SOP – Discipline

Breaking SOP may result in:

  • Warning
  • Strike
  • Suspension
  • Termination

Failure to comply with this SOP will result in disciplinary action.
